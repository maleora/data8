Rajasekhara Charitramu

great

Kandukuri Veereshalingam (కందుకూరి వీరేశలింగం) (16 April 1848 – 27 May 1919), also known as Veereshalingam Pantulu (వీరేశలింగం పంతులు), was a social reformer of Andhra Pradesh. He is widely considered as the man who first brought about a renaissance in Telugu people and Telugu literature. . He was influenced by the ideals of Brahmo Samaj particularly those of Keshub Chunder Sen. He got involved in the cause of social reforms. In 1876 he started a Telugu journal and wrote the first prose for women. He encouraged education for women, and started a school in Dowlaiswaram in 1874.
